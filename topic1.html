Computer Science as a Discipline

Computer science is the systematic study of automated computation. It became a formal discipline in the late 1950s and early 1960s, originating from the efforts of electrical engineers and mathematicians who sought to build devices capable of mechanical computation. (Kaiser, D., n.d.)

It focuses on the development and testing of software and software systems. This involves working with mathematical models, algorithms, data analysis, security, and computational theory. Computer scientists define the computational principles that form the foundation of all software.

5 Computing Disciplines and Majors

  • Computer Engineering
  • Computer Science
  • Information Systems
  • Information Technology
  • Software Engineering

Computer Engineering

Definition

Computer engineering combines principles of computer science and electrical engineering to design and develop software and hardware systems. (Staff, 2025)

History

Computer Engineering emerged in the mid-20th century. Jesiek (2013) traces its development between the 1940s and 1950s when engineering expanded to include computer hardware design. Its growth accelerated during World War II and continues to shape technology today.

Computer Science

Definition

Computer science is the study of computers, computational systems, algorithms, artificial intelligence, cybersecurity, and software development. It combines theoretical foundations with hands-on experience.

History

Computer science became an independent discipline in the early 1960s, although digital computers were invented earlier. Its roots lie in mathematics, electrical engineering, physics, and management information systems.

Information Systems

Definition

Information Systems (IS) are integrated components for collecting, storing, processing, and providing information. Businesses rely on IS for operations, customer interactions, and competition.

History

From 1950s transaction systems to cloud computing and AI today, IS evolved through mainframes, Decision Support Systems (DSS), personal computers, Executive Information Systems (EIS), and internet-based technologies.

Information Technology

Definition

Information Technology (IT) uses computers, networks, and other digital systems to store, process, and exchange data. It plays a major role in business operations.

Types of IT

  • Internet and web technologies
  • Cloud computing
  • Databases
  • AI & Machine Learning
  • Cybersecurity
  • Internet of Things
  • Data Analytics

Software Engineering

Definition

Software engineering is the process of designing, developing, testing, and maintaining software. It uses various tools and methodologies across development.

History

Born from the “software crisis” of the 1960s, software engineering grew into a structured discipline after the 1968 NATO conference. Margaret Hamilton coined the term during the Apollo missions. Development evolved from waterfall models to agile methodologies in the 1990s.

Analysis & Reaction

Computer Science plays a major role in today’s digital society. With new devices emerging every year, the field continues to shape how we work, communicate, and innovate.

As a BSIT student, I see how each discipline contributes differently to technology. These lessons deepen my appreciation of IT and inspire me to carry this knowledge forward.
